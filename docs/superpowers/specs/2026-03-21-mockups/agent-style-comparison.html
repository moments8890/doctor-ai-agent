Traditional Portal vs AI Agent-Centric

How should the patient interact with post-visit features?

Traditional Portal (tabs + screens)
Patient navigates between pages:
📋 Diagnosis Page
Static view of confirmed diagnosis
💊 Treatment Plan Page
Checklist of medications, follow-ups
📤 Upload Page
Upload lab results, imaging
💬 Messages Page
Inbox with doctor replies
Patient must know WHERE to go for each action
AI Agent-Centric (conversation IS the portal)
AI agent surfaces everything contextually:

Patient just talks — AI handles routing, display, triage

AI Agent-Centric Model: How it works

🤖 Proactive
AI pushes info when it matters: "Doctor confirmed your diagnosis", "Time for your follow-up", "Please upload MRI results"
🎯 Contextual
Structured cards (diagnosis, treatment, tasks) appear INSIDE the conversation, not in separate pages
🔀 Triage
AI answers routine questions itself. Escalates to doctor when clinical judgment needed. Patient doesn't decide routing.
📎 Inline Actions
Upload, report symptoms, view plans — all triggered via buttons in AI messages. No navigation needed.

What about non-chat features?

Even agent-centric portals need some structured views. Pick your balance:

A

Pure Agent — conversation only

Single screen: the AI chat. Everything happens in conversation. "Show my records" → agent renders card. "What's my treatment plan?" → agent shows checklist inline.

Like: ChatGPT model — one conversation, rich cards
Risk: Hard to find past info buried in chat history. No persistent treatment checklist to glance at.

B

Agent + Reference drawer

Primary: AI conversation. Secondary: swipe-up drawer or tab with persistent views (treatment checklist, visit history). Agent can deep-link into the drawer.

Like: WeChat mini-program pattern — chat front + structured back
Best of both: Agent handles actions + triage. Drawer for "let me check my treatment plan again" without asking the AI.

C

Agent + Minimal tabs

Two tabs: AI (primary) | My Health (visit history + treatment plan + records). AI tab is the default landing. My Health is the passive reference.

Like: Current structure evolved — Chat tab becomes smart agent, Records tab becomes visit-centric health view
Pragmatic: Least change from existing code. Clear separation.

My Recommendation: B (Agent + Reference Drawer)

Why: Pure agent (A) loses persistent reference — patients will want to re-check their treatment plan without re-asking. Tabs (C) splits the experience and makes the agent feel like "just another chat." The drawer model (B) makes the AI the undisputed front door while giving patients a persistent place for structured data they need to reference repeatedly. It also matches WeChat's interaction patterns (chat-first, swipe for more). The agent proactively pushes info; the drawer is where it lives permanently.
